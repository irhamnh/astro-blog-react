Now, I'm not the best person to say this, and this post is based on my personal believe and experience so take it with a grain of salt.
For me personally, the thing that usually keep me from doing meaningful work is procastination.

This made me question myself, why am I always procrastinate? I could just open my IDE and then stare at it for hours without getting any job done, I'm sure I wasn't always like this.
After months, I started to notice some pattern.

<!-- I always waited for the perfect opportunity, the perfect timing, tried to make everything more effective and efficient before actually doing anything. -->

Unable to do works that I could normally finish in under 1 hour(without AI), got me really thinking. What changed? I wasn't always like this before. After listening to professional and some reading, I found out that this is actually common nowadays, and what is the cause of it.

Dopamine, too much instant dopamine which weaken our dopamine receptor and make us unable to stay focused.

If this is also the case for you, I only have 1 advice. Stop excessively scrolling through your phone, wether it's social media, youtube, etc.
Also, another trick what worked for me is that I stopped listening to music/podcast while working.
